Pāḷi English Dictionary

[EDITED ENTRY]

 

Sukha: (adj.-n.) agreeable, pleasant, blest Vin I.3...; paṭipadā, pleasant path, easy progress A II.149 sq....; kaṇṇa-s. pleasant to the ear D I.4; happy, pleased D II.233. - nt. sukhaṃ wellbeing, happiness, ease; ideal, success Vin I.294; D I.73 sq.; M I.37; S I.5; A III.355 (deva-manussānaṃ)...(lokiya- worldly happiness). - kāyika sukkha bodily welfare...; sāmisaṅ s. material happiness A I.81; III.412.... On relation to pīti (joy) see Vism 145 (saṅkhāra-kkhandha-sangahitā pīti, vedanā-kkhandha-sangahitaṃ sukhaṃ).... - Defined further at Vism 145 and 461 (iṭṭha-phoṭṭhabb-a-nubhavana-lakkhaṇaṃ; i. e. of the kind of experiencing pleasant contacts). - Two kinds, viz. kāyika and cetasika at Ps I.188; several other pairs at A I.80; three (praise, wealth, heaven)...; another three (manussa-, dibba-, nibbāna-)...; four (possessing, making good use of possessions, having no debts, living a blameless life) A II.69. - gātha-bandhana-sukh'atthaṃ for the beauty of the verse J II.224. - Opp. asukha D III.222, 246...; or dukkha, with which often combd.... - Cases: instr. sukhena with comfort, happily, through happiness...; acc. sukhaṃ comfortably, in happiness; yathā s. according to liking...; sukhaṃ seti to rest in ease, to lie well S I.41; A I.136... Cp. sukhasayita. - s. edhati to thrive, prosper S I.217.... s. viharati to live happily, A I.96; III.3...
