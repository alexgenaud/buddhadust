Upekkhā and Upekhā: (f.)[fr. upa + īk.s, cp. Bsk. upek.sā] "looking on", hedonic neutrality or indifference, zero point between joy and sorrow, disinterestedness, neutral feeling, equanimity. Sometimes equivalent to adukkham-asukha-vedana "feeling which is neither pain nor pleasure". D 138 (-sati-parisuddhi purity of mindfulness which comes of disinterestedness cp. Vin III.4; M I.79, 364; III 219; S iv.71, 114 sq., v.209 sq (-indriya); A I 42; 81 (-sukha), 256 (-nimitta); III.185, 291 (-cetovimutti); iv.47 sq., 70 sq., 300, 443; v. 301, 360; Sn 67, 73, 972, 1107, (-sati-saŋuddha)...
