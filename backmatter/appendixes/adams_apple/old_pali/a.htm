The New Shorter Oxford English Dictionary:  the first letter of the modern English alphabet and of the ancient Roman one, corresponding to Greek alpha, Hebrew aleph. The sound originally represented by the letter, in English as in Latin, was a low back vowel, articulated with the tongue as low as possible in the mouth, considerable separation of the jaws, and spreading of the lips.
