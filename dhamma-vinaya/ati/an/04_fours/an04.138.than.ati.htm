Aṇguttara Nikāya
4. Catukka Nipāta
XIV: Puggala Vagga

Sutta 138

Nikaṭṭha Puggala Suttaṃ

Withdrawn

Translated from the Pali by Thanissaro Bhikkhu.
Sourced from the edition at dhammatalks.org

For free distribution only.

 

 

[1][pts] "Monks, there are these four persons to be found existing in the world.

Which four?

The one withdrawn in body but not withdrawn in mind, the one not withdrawn in body but withdrawn in mind, the one not withdrawn in body and not withdrawn in mind, and the one withdrawn in body and withdrawn in mind.

"And how is a person withdrawn in body but not withdrawn in mind?

There is the case where a certain person resorts to isolated wilderness and forest dwellings.

There he thinks thoughts of sensuality, he thinks thoughts of ill will, he thinks thoughts of harmfulness.

This is how a person is withdrawn in body but not withdrawn in mind.

"And how is a person not withdrawn in body but withdrawn in mind?

There is the case where a certain person doesn’t resort to isolated wilderness and forest dwellings.

He thinks thoughts of renunciation, he thinks thoughts of non-ill will, he thinks thoughts of harmlessness.

This is how a person is not withdrawn in body but withdrawn in mind.

"And how is a person not withdrawn in body and not withdrawn in mind?

There is the case where a certain person doesn’t resort to isolated wilderness and forest dwellings.

He thinks thoughts of sensuality, he thinks thoughts of ill will, he thinks thoughts of harmfulness.

This is how a person is not withdrawn in body and not withdrawn in mind.

"And how is a person withdrawn in body and withdrawn in mind?

There is the case where a certain person resorts to isolated wilderness and forest dwellings.

There he thinks thoughts of renunciation, he thinks thoughts of non-ill will, he thinks thoughts of harmlessness.

This is how a person is withdrawn in body and withdrawn in mind.

"These, monks, are four persons to be found existing in the world."
