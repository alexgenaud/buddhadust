Sutta 39

Paṭhama Anu-Dhamma Suttaɱ

In Accordance with the Dhamma 1

[40] [882]

[1][pts][than][olds] At Sāvatthī.

"Bhikkhus, when a bhikkhu is practising in accordance with the Dhamma, this is what accords with the Dhamma: he should dwell engrossed in revulsion towards form, feeling, perception, volitional formations, and consciousness.

One who dwells engrossed in revulsion towards form ... and consciousness, fully understands form, feeling, perception, volitional formations, and consciousness.

One who fully understands form ... and consciousness is freed from form, [41] feeling, perception, volitional formations, and consciousness.

He is freed from birth, aging, and death; freed from sorrow, lamentation, pain, displeasure, and despair; freed from suffering, I say."

 

 

Sutta 40

Dutiya Anudhamma Suttaɱ

In Accordance with the Dhamma 2

[1][pts][than][olds] At Sāvatthī.

At Sāvatthī.

"Bhikkhus, when a bhikkhu is practising in accordance with the Dhamma, this is what accords with the Dhamma: he should dwell contemplating impermanence in form ... (as above) ... he is freed from suffering, I say."
