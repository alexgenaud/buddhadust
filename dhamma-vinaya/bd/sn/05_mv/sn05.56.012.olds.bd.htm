II Dhamma-Cakka-Pavattana Vagga

Sutta 12

Dutiya Tathāgatena Vutta Suttaɱ

The Second Sutta Spoken by the Tathāgata

Translated from the Pali
by
Michael M. Olds

 

 

[1][pts][bodh]

Beggars, this previously unheard teaching of
'the aristocrat of truths with regard to pain'
opened my eyes and knowledge sprang up,
wisdom sprang up,
vision sprang up,
the light sprang up.

When, Beggars, it occurred to me that this previously unheard teaching of
'the aristocrat of truths with regard to pain'
must be thoroughly and precisely understood,
must be seen as true,
must be lived,
must be abandoned,
it opened my eyes and knowledge sprang up,
wisdom sprang up,
vision sprang up,
the light sprang up.

When, Beggars, it occurred to me that this previously unheard teaching of
'the aristocrat of truths with regard to pain'
was thoroughly and precisely understood,
was seen as true,
was lived,
was abandoned,
it opened my eyes and knowledge sprang up,
wisdom sprang up,
vision sprang up,
the light sprang up.
