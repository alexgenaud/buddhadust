Aŋguttara Nikāya
X. Dasaka-Nipāta
V. Akkosa Vagga

Sutta 48

Dasa-Dhamma Suttaɱ

Ten Things

Translated from the Pali
by
Michael M. Olds

 

 

[1][pts][than][piya] I HEAR TELL:

Once the Lucky Man Sāvatthī-town revisiting.

There he addressed the beggars, saying:

"Beggars!"

And "Bhante," they replied

Then the Lucky Man said this to them:

2. "Ten, beggars, are things
on which one who has gone forth should repeatedly reflect.

What ten?

3. 'Castless have I now become'.

One who has gone forth should repeatedly reflect on this.

■

4. 'Bound up in the reactions of others is my life'.

One who has gone forth should repeatedly reflect on this.

■

5. 'What is proper for me to do is now different.'

One who has gone forth should repeatedly reflect on this.

■

6. 'Is there now in me any ethical position for which the self would blame me?'

One who has gone forth should repeatedly reflect on this.

■

7. 'Is there now in me any ethical position for which discerning fellows in the godly life would blame me?'

One who has gone forth should repeatedly reflect on this.

■

8. 'All that I love and find pleasurable will become alien, become not so.'

One who has gone forth should repeatedly reflect on this.

■

9. 'Intention[1] is now my inheritance,
intention my lot,
intention my starting point,
whatsoever intent I form
fair or foul
of such I become the remainderman.'

One who has gone forth should repeatedly reflect on this.

■

10. 'In what way do I live as night and day fly by?'

One who has gone forth should repeatedly reflect on this.

■

11. 'Now then, am I highly delighted with my empty hut?'

One who has gone forth should repeatedly reflect on this.

■

12. 'Now then, is there in me any super-human thing,
any sufficiently extraordinary attainment
of aristocratic-knowledge-and-vision
such that no trouble will beset me
if questioned at the last moment
by those of the godly life?'

One who has gone forth should repeatedly reflect on this.

■

13. These, beggars, are the ten things
on which one who has gone forth should repeatedly reflect.

 

[1] Kamma.
